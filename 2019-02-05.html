I suppose that with nothing else on the site yet, it makes sense to talk about me for a minute. I don't like going on about myself so I'll just stick to the facts.

So right now, today, I am a systems engineer professionally, and privately software development has consumed all my other hobbies for the last 2-3 months. It seems like it's here to stay this time, and I couldn't be happier about it. I attribute that largely to discovering an affinity for working with Python.

My very first full-on programming experience was with Java in an intermediate object-oriented course I took in college. Saying nothing of the language itself, (okay just one - verbosity matters) it's hard to support a language acquired by oracle and then used to sue Google all the way to the supreme court, threatening a change in legal precedent which could fundamentally change how developers (and, by extenstion, users) work.
